Which national park was the first one ever established?
Yellowstone National Park, founded in 1872.
How many people work for the National Park service?
20,000 employees and over 300,000 volunteers.
What is the average fiscal year budget for national parks over the past 5 years?
$2.84 billion
Which national park is the largest?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres.
Which national park is the smallest?
Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA, at 0.02 acres.
How many yearly visitors come to the national parks?
Nearly 331,000,000.
Which parks are the most visited?
Great Smoky Mountains (9 million guests per year) and Grand Canyon (4 million guests per year).
Which state(s) are home to the most national parks?
California and Alaska (8).
Which park is the least visited?
Kobuk Valley National Park (3,000 visitors per year).
Are all national parks completely "natural"?
No. Mesa Verde National Park was the first park to be recognized as man-made, as some of the rock has been scuplted by Native Americans. Alcatraz is a former prison site and Molokai Island was a former leper colony.
