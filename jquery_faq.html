Are all national parks completely "natural"?
No. Mesa Verde National Park was the first park to be recognized as man-made, as some of the rock has been scuplted by Native Americans. Alcatraz is a former prison site and Molokai Island was a former leper colony.

Interesting Facts

Death Valley National Park

  • The average yearly rainfall is 2.36 inches.
  • The park was named by gold settlers in the 1800s as they tried to find their treasure by crossing the desolate wasteland.
  • Death Valley was a famous filming location of the Star Wars films.
  • The park holds the record as the hottest place on earth, recording a "deathly" temperature of 134 degrees Fahrenheit in 1913.

Alcatraz National Park

  • Notorious gangster Al Capone was one of the first inmates.
  • There were no confirmed escapes from the prison; 36 people tried but were either captured, shot, or drowned.
  • The island was named after the amount of pelicans ("Alcatraces" in Spanish) which occupied the area.
  • It was home to the first lighthouse on the Pacific Coast.

Grand Canyon National Park

  • It is bigger than the state of Rhode island.
  • There are estimated to be at least 700 undiscovered caves in the trenches of the park.
  • The most dangerous animal in the park is the rock squirrel, which bites multiple visitors who try to feed it.
  • Visitors may purchase souvenirs but aren't allowed to actually bring them home. This is known as "Leave No Trace".
